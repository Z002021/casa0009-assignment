Introduction

Biodiversity is the name we give to the variety of all the plants, animals, bacteria and fungi with which we share the planet. It is the range of species found in every habitat on Earth. Biodiversity is undoubtedly important for all of us. The processes that from keeping our reservoirs clean to the food in the fields growing are all underpinned by the wildlife - or biodiversity - that surrounds it, and without any of these, other species simply would not be able to survive. It is not the mere presence of these species that matters most but their relationships with each other and how they interact to create a complex network of life. However, as humans change the environment, from building roads to digging up fields, we chip away at this diversity of life, reducing the number of species that are found in any one place. This is causing species around the planet to decline at a concerning speed. In this project, we will investigate the situation of biodiversity in England and try to provide you more valuable information.

Overview

In this part, we use three indicators - occurrences density (Occ. Density), species density (Sp. Density), and species density (Sp. Evenness) - to reflect the level of biodiversity in England.

Endangered Species

In this part, we will look at the distribution of 25 critically endangered species in England during 2012 and 2021 and how the amount have changed.

Zoom in to London

For this part, a two-step clustering was used to identify and characterise the biodiversity hotspots in London. OPTICS was fist performed to identify hotspots with high density of occurrences. K-Means clustering was then used to categorise the biodiversity quality of each hotspots (i.e., species richness density and species evenness).
